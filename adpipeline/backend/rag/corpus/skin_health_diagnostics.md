# Colgate Skin Health Portfolio — Diagnostic

Provenance: impairment figures and portfolio facts are REAL public data from
Colgate-Palmolive filings and earnings releases; competitive reads are internal
analysis for this demo.

## Portfolio (real)
Colgate-Palmolive's Skin Health business includes premium/derm brands acquired to
diversify beyond oral and home care: Filorga (French anti-aging skincare, acquired
2019 for approximately $1.7 billion), plus PCA Skin and EltaMD
(professional/clinical skincare, both acquired in 2018).

## Growth stalled — the impairment record (real)
- Q4 2023: Colgate-Palmolive recorded a non-cash, after-tax impairment charge of
  $620 million to adjust the carrying values of goodwill and intangible assets of
  the Filorga skin health business.
- Q4 2025: a further non-cash, after-tax impairment of $794 million on skin health
  goodwill and intangibles, citing lower-than-expected category growth rates and
  weaker-than-expected performance, particularly in China.
The anticipated premium prestige-beauty ramp never materialized at plan; long-term
growth and margin expectations for the unit have been cut twice.

## China / APAC exposure
A large share of the original Filorga thesis rested on Chinese prestige-beauty demand
(travel retail + cross-border e-commerce). That demand softened sharply. The brands
carry near-zero brand equity versus digitally-native insurgent skincare labels that
dominate China/APAC social commerce and marketplace discovery.

## Competitive gap
Insurgent, derm-led skincare brands out-execute on content velocity, marketplace SEO,
and KOL/live-commerce activation. CeraVe (L'Oréal) is the reference case: launched in
India only in 2023 and already growing well ahead of the market (see
competitor_snapshot.md). Filorga/PCA Skin remain reliant on legacy prestige and
professional channels and are structurally slow on digital-native demand generation.

## Implications
Any Palmolive/skin-health marketing motion must assume a standing start on digital
brand equity in APAC, prioritize marketplace + social-commerce discovery, and avoid
over-investing in channels where these brands have no defensible position.
