# Colgate Skin Health Portfolio — Diagnostic

## Portfolio
Colgate-Palmolive's Skin Health business includes premium/derm brands acquired to
diversify beyond oral and home care, notably Filorga (French anti-aging skincare)
and PCA Skin (professional/clinical skincare).

## Growth stalled
Skin Health growth has stalled. After the acquisitions, the anticipated premium
prestige-beauty ramp did not materialize at plan. The company recorded a significant
non-cash asset impairment charge tied to the Skin Health / Filorga reporting unit,
reflecting lowered long-term growth and margin expectations.

## China / APAC exposure
A large share of the original Filorga thesis rested on Chinese prestige-beauty demand
(travel retail + cross-border e-commerce). That demand softened sharply. The brands
carry near-zero brand equity versus digitally-native insurgent skincare labels that
dominate China/APAC social commerce and marketplace discovery.

## Competitive gap
Insurgent D2C skincare brands out-execute on content velocity, marketplace SEO, and
KOL/live-commerce activation. Filorga/PCA Skin remain reliant on legacy prestige and
professional channels and are structurally slow on digital-native demand generation.

## Implications
Any Palmolive/skin-health marketing motion must assume a standing start on digital
brand equity in APAC, prioritize marketplace + social-commerce discovery, and avoid
over-investing in channels where these brands have no defensible position.
