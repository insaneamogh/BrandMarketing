# Competitor Snapshot

Provenance: category sizing and dated competitor moves are REAL public data
(industry reports, 2024–2025); positioning reads are internal analysis for this demo.

## Category context (real)
Global retail sales of pet food, treats and related products reached about
$200 billion in 2024, growing 1.6% — a huge but maturing category. Nestlé Purina
PetCare and Mars Petcare have the two largest revenues in pet food worldwide;
Hill's ($4.5B, FY2024) competes as the science/therapeutic specialist, not on scale.

## Pet Nutrition (vs Hill's)
- **Royal Canin (Mars):** breed/condition-specific precision-nutrition positioning;
  very strong vet-channel relationships globally, including deeper APAC penetration
  than Hill's. Premium price, science-forward. Main threat in vet/specialty.
  Hill's Prescription Diet and Royal Canin Veterinary Diet are the two dominant
  players in the high-margin, clinically differentiated therapeutic segment.
- **Purina Pro Plan (Nestlé):** broad availability (mass + specialty + Amazon),
  aggressive sports/performance and life-stage marketing, strong e-commerce and
  subscribe-and-save execution. Out-spends on paid social. In 2024 Purina put its
  Pro Plan Veterinary Diets range (30+ prescription diets) on Amazon — a direct
  move into the vet-channel moat. Main threat on reach and e-commerce.

## Skin / Personal Care (vs Palmolive / Skin Health)
- **Dove (Unilever):** "real beauty" equity, moisturization credibility, huge mass
  distribution and share of voice. Benchmark for warmth + inclusivity messaging.
- **Olay (P&G):** anti-aging science positioning, strong mass-prestige bridge and
  retail media muscle. Benchmark for efficacy storytelling done compliantly.
- **CeraVe (L'Oréal):** derm-recommended, ingredient-led (ceramides), explosive
  social/UGC-driven growth and marketplace SEO. Launched in India only in 2023
  under L'Oréal's Dermatological Beauty division — the group's fastest-growing
  division — and is growing well ahead of the market in emerging markets. This is
  the insurgent playbook Skin Health brands have failed to match; dominant in
  exactly the digital-native space where Filorga/PCA Skin are weak.

## Positioning reads
- Hill's differentiator vs Royal Canin/Purina: therapeutic credibility + Youthful
  Vitality senior story; must not cede e-commerce/Amazon to Purina, especially now
  that Purina sells veterinary diets there directly.
- Palmolive/Skin Health must study CeraVe's content velocity + marketplace SEO rather
  than compete on legacy prestige where CeraVe/Olay/Dove already win.
