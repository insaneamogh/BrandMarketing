# Therapeutic / Prescription Pet Diet Market (REAL)

Provenance: REAL public data (industry market research, 2025; Hill's product
announcements). Directly supports Hill's Prescription Diet (k/d Kidney Care and
the wider Rx line) — the portfolio's highest-margin product family.

## Market size and growth
- The US pet therapeutic diet market is roughly $1.9 billion in 2025 and is
  projected to reach $3.6 billion by 2034 (~7.2% CAGR) — growing far faster than
  the overall pet food category (+1.6% in 2024).
- Globally, the pet veterinary diet market is about $27 billion in 2025, heading
  to roughly $38.7 billion by 2030 (~7.4% CAGR).

## Channel structure
- More than 65% of therapeutic pet diet sales originate in the veterinary
  channel: most products (Hill's Prescription Diet, Purina Pro Plan Veterinary
  Diets, Royal Canin Veterinary Diet) require a vet recommendation or
  prescription, making clinics the primary point of purchase.
- This matches internal data: the "Vet Recommended" campaign is the highest-ROAS
  flight on record (4.6x — campaign_history.md), and vet referral has the highest
  conversion rate of any channel (12.0% — channel_metrics.md) but is
  capacity-constrained by clinic throughput.

## Hill's position
- Hill's Prescription Diet is the leading US vet-recommended therapeutic line and
  Hill's dominates the veterinary recommendation channel through practitioner
  education programs.
- Innovation cadence is active: January 2024 brought new Prescription Diet
  products and enhancements; in March 2025 Hill's launched ONC Care, a
  prescription diet supporting food intake for canine and feline oncology
  patients.
- Therapeutic + wellness categories led Hill's +4.9% organic growth in 2024
  (hills_regional_sales.md).

## Implications for marketing k/d Kidney Care
Demand generation should feed the vet channel (referral programs, clinic
education, condition-awareness content for pet parents) rather than fight it;
e-commerce (Amazon) is the growth flank now that competitors sell veterinary
diets there directly (competitor_snapshot.md). Compliance note: therapeutic
claims require veterinary substantiation and approved language only — see
brand_guidelines_hills.md BANNED CLAIMS.
