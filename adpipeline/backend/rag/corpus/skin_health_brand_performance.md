# Skin Health Brands — EltaMD vs Filorga Performance (REAL + MOCK)

Provenance: brand facts and acquisition history are REAL public data
(Colgate-Palmolive announcements, press coverage); the campaign readings quote
the internal MOCK flights in campaign_history.md. This file is the head-to-head
of the portfolio's best performer (EltaMD) and its problem child (Filorga).

## EltaMD — the quiet winner (real)
- EltaMD is the #1 dermatologist-recommended professional sunscreen brand in the
  United States; UV Clear Broad-Spectrum SPF 46 is its hero SKU.
- Acquired by Colgate-Palmolive in December 2017 (together with PCA Skin;
  combined net sales were roughly $100 million at acquisition) and has compounded
  strongly since — repeatedly credited by management as a growth driver within
  Skin Health while Filorga shrank.
- Distribution is derm-office professional channel + DTC + Amazon; the brand owns
  a credible medical ritual (it founded National Dermatologist Day, celebrated
  with an NYSE bell ringing) and keeps winning consumer beauty awards (Allure
  Best of Beauty 2025, InStyle 2025 Readers' Choice).
- The playbook mirrors Hill's: professional-endorsement credibility (dermatologist
  ≈ veterinarian) converted into premium price and repeat purchase.

## Filorga — the stalled prestige bet (real)
- Filorga (French anti-aging skincare, hero line NCEF-Reverse) was acquired in
  2019 for ~$1.7 billion; the thesis rested on Chinese prestige-beauty demand
  that then softened sharply.
- Two impairments followed: $620 million after-tax (Q4 2023) and a further
  $794 million (Q4 2025) — see skin_health_diagnostics.md for the full record.
- The brand is structurally weak in digital-native discovery (content velocity,
  marketplace SEO, KOL/live-commerce) exactly where CeraVe wins.

## Internal campaign readings (MOCK — see campaign_history.md)
- EltaMD "Derm Counter Trust" (derm-office referral + Amazon): ROAS 4.4x, CPA
  $18 — the skin-health portfolio's scale candidate.
- EltaMD "Skinfluencer UGC" (TikTok + Meta): ROAS 3.4x, CTR 3.2% — creator
  content works when fronted by real dermatologists.
- Filorga "Prestige Counter Revival" (EU travel retail + Meta): ROAS 0.8x —
  below break-even; legacy counter placements do not create discovery.
- Filorga "Tmall Global Reboot" (China cross-border): ROAS 1.1x — below
  break-even; near-zero digital brand equity vs insurgent labels.

## Implication
Fund EltaMD's derm-credibility engine (referral + UGC + Amazon) and stop
funding Filorga prestige placements until a digital-native discovery motion
(derm-adjacent creators, marketplace SEO) exists. Filorga spend should be
diagnostic-sized, not campaign-sized.
