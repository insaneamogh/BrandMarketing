# Hill's Pet Nutrition: Regional & Financial Snapshot

Provenance: the "Reported financials" section is REAL public data from Colgate-Palmolive's
FY2024 results (Form 10-K / Q4 2024 earnings release). Regional reads and implications are
internal analyst interpretation layered on top for this demo.

## Reported financials (FY2024, real)
- Hill's Pet Nutrition net sales: $4,483 million in 2024, up 4.5% year over year
  (volume +0.8%, net selling prices +4.1%, foreign exchange -0.4%).
- Organic sales grew +4.9% in 2024, led by the United States and by the
  therapeutic and wellness categories.
- The deliberate exit from private-label pet food manufacturing reduced Hill's
  organic sales growth by roughly 210 basis points - headline volume looks weaker
  even as branded mix improves.
- Operating profit: $965 million in 2024, up 20%, a 21.5% operating margin.
- Scale within the portfolio: Hill's is roughly 22% of Colgate-Palmolive's ~$20.1
  billion total net sales - the single largest non-oral-care growth engine, and the
  fastest-compounding segment over the trailing five years, though growth has
  decelerated from pandemic-era peaks.

## Regional performance (internal read)
- North America and Europe are the strongest markets, posting roughly 6-7% organic
  sales growth, led by Science Diet and the Prescription Diet therapeutic line.
- Asia-Pacific (APAC) and Latin America (LatAm) lag materially: low brand awareness,
  thin specialty-channel presence, and strong local competitors constrain share.
- Reported volume is dragged down by the private-label exit noted above.

## Channel dependence
Hill's is heavily dependent on the veterinary and specialty-pet channel. Prescription
Diet is sold through vet clinics and endorsed by veterinarians; Science Diet skews to
pet specialty retail (e.g., Petco, independent pet stores). This vet/specialty
concentration is a strength in NA/EU but a structural weakness in APAC/LatAm where
that infrastructure is fragmented or absent.

## Implications
Growth headroom is concentrated in under-penetrated APAC/LatAm markets and in
e-commerce, where Hill's is under-indexed versus mass-market competitors. Youthful
Vitality (7+ senior formula) is a priority premium innovation for the senior-pet
demographic in developed markets - see senior_pet_demographics.md: more than half of
US dogs are now age 7 or older, so the 7+ audience is the majority of the market,
not a niche.
