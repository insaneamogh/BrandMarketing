# Palmolive — Channel & Distribution Data

Provenance: the "India market sizing" section is REAL public data (industry market
reports, 2025). Channel structure and implications are internal analysis for this demo.

## Core channels
Palmolive (personal care: bar/liquid hand soap, shower gel, dish liquid in some
markets) sells predominantly through mass grocery and drug retail. Mass grocery and
drug together account for the large majority of Palmolive personal-care volume in
established markets, a classic high-distribution, low-price-point CPG profile.

## India market sizing (real)
- The India body wash market was valued at roughly USD 81 million in 2025 and is
  projected to more than double to about USD 172 million by 2034 (~8.8% CAGR) —
  bar-to-liquid conversion is still early, so category headroom is large.
- Within bath & shower, the shower gel segment is growing at the highest CAGR of
  any sub-category, historically expanding at over 18% per year.
- Premiumization is the defining trend: consumers are trading up from mass products
  to options with better ingredients, richer fragrance, and a sensorial experience.
  India's luxury bath & body segment alone is about USD 1.5 billion (FY2025),
  heading to roughly USD 2.4 billion by FY2033.

## Quick-commerce shift
Quick-commerce (10–30 minute delivery) share is rising fastest in India and Latin
America. In India, platforms like Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart are taking a growing
slice of impulse and top-up personal-care purchases. Quick-commerce buyers skew
urban, younger, and convenience-led, and respond to bundle offers and instant
availability more than to brand storytelling. Full market data with real GMV and
platform-share figures: quick_commerce_india.md.

## Regional notes
- North America / Europe: mature, promotion-driven, private-label pressure on value tiers.
- India: quick-commerce + modern trade growth; strong price sensitivity; large TAM.
- Latin America: quick-commerce emerging in metros; traditional trade still dominant
  outside major cities.

## Premium line
The "Luminous Oils" range positions above core Palmolive on ingredient story
(natural oils, indulgent fragrance). It rides the premiumization trend above, but
needs channels that support a slightly premium price and richer creative than a
commodity soap SKU — modern trade end-caps, marketplace A+ content, and social-led
discovery rather than pure price promotion.

## Implications
Match spend to where the premium buyer actually converts: marketplace + modern trade
in India/LatAm metros, and social discovery. Avoid deep discounting that collapses
the premium positioning.
