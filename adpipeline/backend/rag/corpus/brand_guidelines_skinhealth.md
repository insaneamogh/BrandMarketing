# Skin Health (EltaMD / Filorga / PCA Skin): Brand & Creative Guidelines

Provenance: internal creative guardrails (demo). BANNED CLAIMS are hard compliance
rules - they override every other instruction, including human feedback.

## Tone
- EltaMD: clinical confidence, calm and trustworthy - "recommended by the people
  who study skin." Minimal, science-literate, never hyped. Speak to daily
  protection as a habit, not a luxury.
- Filorga: French aesthetic-medicine heritage, refined and precise. Premium but
  modern - avoid dusty "prestige counter" tropes; the brand must feel like a
  discovery, not an heirloom.

## Visual rules
- EltaMD: white/neutral clinical backdrops, natural daylight, real diverse skin
  (all tones - UV Clear must read as universal); the pump bottle is the hero,
  always legible. Teal accent may echo the label. No beach clichés as the only
  story - daily-wear contexts (commute, office, outdoors) perform better.
- Filorga: black/white/gold palette, glass-jar hero shots with texture (cream
  swirl, cross-section), Parisian-apothecary cues. Elevated but crisp lighting;
  never dim "luxury gloom".
- Both: skin shown honestly - no unrealistic retouching; texture and pores are
  allowed and build derm credibility.

## BANNED CLAIMS (hard compliance: never generate)
- No disease prevention/treatment claims beyond approved sunscreen monograph
  language (e.g., never "prevents skin cancer" or "cures acne/eczema/rosacea").
- No "100% protection", "all-day protection without reapplying", or waterproof
  claims (US labeling rules: water-resistant with a stated duration only).
- No anti-aging cure or "reverses aging" language (Filorga especially: firmness/
  wrinkle-appearance claims must stay cosmetic, not medical).
- No "chemical-free" / "100% natural" absolutes.
- No implied physician/dermatologist endorsement without substantiation. NOTE:
  "#1 dermatologist-recommended professional sunscreen brand" IS substantiated
  for EltaMD and may be used verbatim for EltaMD only - never generalized to
  other products or paraphrased into "doctors say...".
- No comparative disparagement of named competitors.

## Approved angles
Daily protection as self-respect, derm-trusted credibility (substantiated),
sensitive/acne-prone suitability described cosmetically, ingredient
transparency (zinc oxide, niacinamide, NCEF complex), visible-quality-of-skin
outcomes in cosmetic terms, French aesthetic-medicine heritage (Filorga).
