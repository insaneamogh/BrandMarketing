# Industry Ad Benchmarks — Meta & Amazon (REAL, 2025)

Provenance: REAL public benchmark data (WordStream/LocaliQ 2025 Facebook Ads
benchmark study of 1,000+ campaigns; published Amazon Ads benchmark reports).
Use these to sanity-check the internal MOCK figures in channel_metrics.md and
campaign_history.md and to calibrate expected-metric probabilities.

## Meta (Facebook/Instagram) — 2025 cross-industry benchmarks
- Median CTR: 2.19% — Meta's visual formats still deliver strong top-of-funnel
  engagement.
- Median CPM: $13.48; median CPA across all industries: $38.17.
- Average CPC: $0.70 on traffic-objective campaigns; $1.92 on leads-objective
  campaigns.
- Facebook Lead Ads: average conversion rate ~7.72%, average cost per lead $27.66
  across industries.
- Beauty & personal care is among the MOST expensive verticals on Meta at ~$3.06
  average CPC (third highest of all tracked industries) — relevant to any
  Palmolive paid-social plan.

## Amazon Ads — 2025 benchmarks
- Average ACOS (ad cost of sales): roughly 29% platform-wide.
- Beauty & personal care: typical ACOS 18–28%; category conversion rate ~14.6%.
- Pet supplies: typical ACOS 20–32%; category conversion rate ~15.3%; high
  repeat-purchase lifetime value justifies a higher initial ACOS in pet.
- Sponsored Products CTR typically runs 0.35–0.70%.
- Amazon platform-average conversion rate: 10–12% — an order of magnitude above
  paid social, because shoppers arrive with purchase intent.

## How to read these against the internal (MOCK) numbers
- Internal Meta CPLs in channel_metrics.md ($3.10–$8.40) are LOWER than the public
  $27.66 lead-ads median because the internal "lead" definition is lighter
  (add-to-cart, email signup) — compare direction and ratios, not levels.
- Internal Amazon CVR (1.9–2.1%) measures click-to-purchase on cold paid traffic;
  the public 10–12% platform figure includes branded high-intent search. Both data
  sets support the same conclusion: Amazon converts far better than paid social
  for CPG, in both pet and beauty categories.
- Campaign CTRs in campaign_history.md (0.7%–3.4%) bracket the public Meta median
  of 2.19%, and the Amazon Always-On CTR (0.9%) beats the public Sponsored
  Products norm (0.35–0.70%) — the internal history is realistic.
