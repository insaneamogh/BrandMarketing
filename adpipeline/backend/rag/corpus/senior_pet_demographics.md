# Senior-Pet Demographics — The 7+ Opportunity (REAL)

Provenance: REAL public data (US pet-ownership surveys and industry research,
Packaged Facts / Freedonia, 2022–2024 vintages). Directly supports targeting for
Hill's Youthful Vitality (7+ senior formula).

## The US pet population is aging
- More than 50% of US dogs and cats are now age 7 or older — the threshold most
  veterinarians and food formulations treat as "senior."
- Among dog-owning households, the share owning a senior dog rose from 41.6% in
  2012 to 53.5% in 2022; roughly 26.5 million US households own a senior dog.
- Puppies are a shrinking share: only 8% of dog owners had a puppy under one year
  old in 2024, down from 11% in 2018.

## What senior-pet owners buy
- Senior/mature formulations are among the most-used specialized pet foods: about
  19% of dog owners feed one.
- Adjacent senior-wellness categories — joint supplements, cognitive-health foods,
  orthopedic beds — have multiplied over the past decade, signaling owners' rising
  willingness to spend on aging-pet quality of life.

## Implication for Hill's Youthful Vitality (7+)
The addressable base for a 7+ senior formula is now the MAJORITY of US dog-owning
households and still growing — senior-pet messaging is the center of the market,
not a niche. This demographic tailwind is consistent with internal results: the
"Senior Vitality" campaign (ROAS 3.1x) and "7+ Renewal" (ROAS 3.0x) in
campaign_history.md both cleared the portfolio's scale bar on senior-pet creative.
Life-stage messaging must stay within brand_guidelines_hills.md limits: vitality
and renewal in wellness terms, never "reverses aging" or cure claims.
