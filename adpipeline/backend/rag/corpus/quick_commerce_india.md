# Quick-Commerce India — Market Data (REAL)

Provenance: REAL public data (platform results and filings, industry reports,
2024–2026). Region: India. This is the channel behind Palmolive's best-performing
campaign (see campaign_history.md, "Quick-Commerce Blitz").

## Market size and growth
- India quick-commerce GMV reached roughly $6–7 billion in 2024 and is forecast to
  compound around 40% annually through 2030.
- By early 2026 the market was clocking about ₹11,000 crore of GMV in a single
  month (~100% growth year over year) on roughly 7.8 million orders per day.

## Platform landscape
- Blinkit (Eternal/Zomato): market leader with more than 50% share as of
  September 2025; gross order value of ₹11,821 crore in the quarter ended June
  2025 — larger than Zomato's food-delivery business itself.
- Zepto: FY25 revenue of ₹11,110 crore, up roughly 150% year over year.
- Swiggy Instamart: Q4 FY25 gross order value of ₹4,670 crore, up 101% year over
  year.

## Category shift toward personal care & beauty
Platforms are expanding well beyond grocery into beauty, personal care, electronics
and seasonal gifting, and pushing into Tier-2/3 cities. Quick-commerce buyers skew
urban, younger and convenience-led; they respond to bundle offers and instant
availability more than to brand storytelling — impulse and top-up personal-care
purchases are migrating to these apps.

## Implication for Palmolive Luminous Oils
The internal data and the public data point the same way: the "Quick-Commerce
Blitz" (Blinkit/Zepto ads) is Palmolive's best ROAS on record at 4.2x with a $6
CPA (campaign_history.md), and internal quick-commerce economics — $2.40 CPL,
4.6% CVR — are the strongest of any India channel (channel_metrics.md). The public
market data above says the channel's ceiling is still rising fast, so scaling into
it rides a ~40%-a-year tailwind. Watch out-of-stock risk: availability, not demand,
capped the earlier "Indulgent Ritual" flight.
